MYTHS

AND PROPAGANDA SPREAD ABOUT ARTSAKH

Azerbaijan uses propaganda and the falsification of history in order to justify the ethnic cleansing of Artsakh, as well as the erasure of sacred Armenian monuments.
One of the most typical and widespread medieval Armenian monuments are khachkars (stone cross). Khachkars were spread across all lands where Armenians lived. Therefore, an important manifestation of the "Albanization" of the Armenian cultural heritage in Azerbaijan's historic revisionism is the theory that the Armenian khachkars of Nagorno-Karabakh (Artsakh), Nakhichevan and Syunik are actually 'Albanian' artifacts under the name "khachdashi" (with replaces of the Armenian word 'kar' (stone), with the Azerbaijani word 'dash', also meaning stone.
MYTH #1 - The Azerbaijani claim that Nagorno-Karabakh (Artsakh) belongs to Azerbaijan.
Artsakh has been inhabited by Armenians for 2500 years, as attested by ancient historians Strabo and Pliny the Elder. In 1991, following Soviet law, Armenians voted in a referendum to secede from the Soviet Union and join Armenia. Azerbaijan attacked Armenian communities in Baku, Sumgait, Kirovabad and Artsakh, leading to mass murder, pogroms, and property confiscation. Armenians in Artsakh successfully defended themselves and won the war in 1994, running a de facto state for nearly 30 years.
MYTH #2 - That there is no precedent for the genocidal markers unfolding today in Armenia and Artsakh
Sumgait, Kirovabad, and Artsakh. After Artsakh declared independence, Azerbaijan violently persecuted the Armenians, leading them to defend themselves. Turkey and Azerbaijan are believed to be committing genocide again, as evidenced by their targeting of civilians, civilian infrastructure, cultural and historical monuments. This is considered a continuation of the Armenian Genocide.
MYTH #3 - Hostilities broke out on September 27 2020, Armenians attacked the Azeris first. That both sides “blame the other” for violating the brokered humanitarian ceasefire agreements.
Armenians are living peacefully in their historic homeland despite being blockaded by hostile forces. Armenia and Artsakh are outmatched by Turkey and Azerbaijan, but evidence shows that Turkey, Azerbaijan, and Israel were planning a military incursion. In August, Turkey and Azerbaijan conducted joint military drills in Baku and Nakhichevan. The Azeri President admitted to initiating the attack on Artsakh. Mainstream media incorrectly report that both sides are to blame for the hostilities instead of acknowledging Azerbaijan's violation of ceasefire agreements. Armenia and Artsakh have requested electronic monitors to identify ceasefire violators, but Azerbaijan has rejected this idea.
MYTH #4 - Turkey did not assist Azerbaijan in this war
Turkey is managing Azerbaijan's military command and directing all airstrikes on Armenian targets, while also blocking humanitarian flights to Armenia. Armenia is not a threat to peace in the region, contrary to Erdogan's beliefs. Armenia and Artsakh are courageously fighting alone against bullies, but mainstream media are not reporting on their bravery.
MYTH #5 - Armenia and Azerbaijan are evenly matched
Turkey and Azerbaijan, with a combined population of 92 million and a GDP of $818 billion, have a vast advantage over Armenia and Artsakh, with 3.1 million people and $12 billion GDP, in a David and Goliath struggle. Mainstream media are urging a ceasefire but not highlighting Armenia's desire for peace and the continued aggression of Turkey, Azerbaijan, and Israel. The use of sophisticated drones by Turkey is causing harm to the civilian population.

Several US PR companies are lobbying for Azerbaijan and presenting it as a victim of Armenian aggression, ignoring its human rights abuses. Azerbaijan has spent $1.3 million for this campaign, hiring firms like Stellar Jay Communications, BGR, and the Podesta Group. The media instead focus on the Armenian Diaspora, presenting it as an unfair advantage for Armenia in the conflict, ignoring the influence of PR campaigns and the background of the Armenian Diaspora resulting from past genocides.
MYTH #8 - Azerbaijan observes international rule of warfare
Turkey and Azerbaijan are importing jihadists and mercenaries to the conflict in Artsakh, and using banned weapons against Armenian civilians, including the destruction of residential, cultural, and religious sites. Azerbaijan is also committing war crimes such as killing elderly civilians and beheading Armenian prisoners of war. Mainstream media is not accurately reporting these actions and is instead focusing on suggesting unverified claims and not mentioning the humane treatment of Azeri prisoners of war by the Armenians.
MYTH #9 - Pakistan is not aiding Azerbaijan
Pakistan has sent troops to fight alongside Turkish, Azeri, and mercenary forces attacking Artsakh. The Armenian Defense Ministry spokesman has said that Armenians fight for their land with their own blood, not with mercenaries like the Turks. Mainstream media has not reported that the casualties sustained by Artsakh are Armenian young men protecting their families and borders or that Turkey is using imported soldiers of fortune as cannon fodder and withholding their passports and stipends.
MYTH #10 - Georgia is not aiding Azerbaijan
Georgia is preventing Armenians in its Akhalkalaki region from sending used tires to Armenia for military insulation, but allows Turkish military free access over its territory by air and land.

A concerted effort to draw Iran into a confrontation with Turkey and neighboring states is underway, with Azerbaijani troops purposely placing themselves in a position where Armenian artillery would violate the Iranian frontier.
MYTH #12 - That Azerbaijan is a bastion of interfaith and cultural tolerance
Azerbaijan and Turkey are on the US State Department's Special Watch List for violating religious freedom. Mainstream media have not highlighted that both countries' educational systems promote discrimination and hatred towards Armenians, nor have they addressed how Turkey and Azerbaijan once scorned Armenian culture but now claim it as their own.
MYTH #13 - Azerbaijan is democratic and honors free speech and assembly
Azerbaijan and Turkey were designated "not free" in Freedom House's "Freedom in the World" 2020 Report, with Azerbaijan experiencing a 10-point decline and Turkey a 31-point decline in freedom over the past decade. Armenia, however, was not listed among the problematic countries and was mentioned as having a promising democratically-elected government.
