Answering all your questions about Artsakh.

December 1920 Narimanov made a declaration giving Nagorno-Karabakh, Nakhichevan and Syunik to Armenia. Azerbaijan, the Bolsheviks & Armenia all agreed. Stalin even published an article celebrating, but Narimanov changed his mind. Russia tried pushing back but he threatened resignation as well as restoring anti-Soviet groups in Azerbaijan.

The Nagorno-Karabakh conflict is the longest-running war in post-Soviet Eurasia. In 1988, ethnic Armenians living in Nagorno-Karabakh demanded the transfer of what was then the Nagorno-Karabakh Autonomous Oblast (NKAO) from Soviet Azerbaijan to Armenia. As the Soviet Union collapsed, tensions grew into an outright war.

The Republic of Artsakh, also known as Nagorno-Karabakh has historically been and continues to be an integral part of Armenia. Dating back to the 9-6th century BC, the region was governed by various Armenian kingdoms, and in the 17th century it was annexed by the Russian Empire.

If Armenia recognises Nagorno Karabakh as an independent state this will isolate Armenia completely and could lead to political, economic and military sanctions. UN member states will vote against this decision.

Azerbaijan has been blocking the only road access for Artsakh to the rest of the world, since the end of 2022. Nagorno-Karabakh is experiencing shortages of food, medicine, and electricity are widespread, and emergency reserves are being rationed. It is widely suspected that the blockade has been orchestrated by the Azerbaijani government as a form of hybrid warfare in its quest to subdue and eventually take control of the region.

Karabakh is the Russian rendering of an Azeri word meaning "black garden", while Nagorno is a Russian root meaning "mountainous". Hence, why The Republic of Artsakh uses the Armenian name "Artsakh".

In the census of 2015, the population of Artsakh had a population of 145,053, consisting of 144,683 Armenians and 238 Russians, and others. It is estimated that the current population of Artsakh now is around 120,000.

In 2020, Armenia and Azerbaijan clashed over the disputed territory of Nagorno Karabakh, with Azerbaijan ultimately reclaiming much of the land liberated by the Armenians the 1990s. More than 6000 people were killed, and thousands more were driven from their homes.

The current phase of Nagorno-Karabakh conflict began in 1988, when in response to the self-determination claims of NK population the Azeri authorities organized massacres and ethnic cleansing of the Armenian population on the entire territory of Azerbaijan, particularly in Sumgait, Baku and Kirovabad.

Under the 2020 ceasefire agreement, Azerbaijan was handed over the seven adjacent bufferzone districts to Nagorno-Karabakh that Armenia held since the 90s. Nagorno-Karabakh however, is governed by the Republic of Artsakh (also known as the Nagorno-Karabakh Republic (NKR)) since the first Nagorno-Karabakh War.

Nagorno-Karabakh is historic Armenian lands which were illegally seized by Azerbaijan. As per the December 1920 agreement, Karabakh along with Nakhichevan were to be given back to Armenia. Which Azerbaijan refused to do so, holding these regions hostage.
